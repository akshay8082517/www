About Indian Onions

Onion (Allium cepa) is one of the important commercial vegetable crops grown in India. It is widely grown in different parts of the country. It is used as salad or cooked in various ways in all curries, fried, boiled or baked. It is also used in processed forms e.g. flakes, powder paste, pickles etc. It has very good medicinal value. Nutritive value of onion varies from variety to variety. Its major value is in its flavour. Onion ranks medium in calorific value, low in protein and very low in vitamins. The perpetual demand of onions within the country and for the export has made it essential to supply onions round the year either from fresh harvest or from stocks. Annual production of onion in India is estimated to be about 5.5 million tonnes. This quantity is enough to meet the present domestic requirement as well as export of onions which is estimated at 1.161 Million Tons for the year 2006-07.
PRODUCTION AND PRODUCTIVITY :- A global review of area and production of major vegetable crops shows that onion ranks second in area under vegetables and third in production in the world. As per FAO 2005 reports, the world production of onion was 57.91 million tonnes from 3.18 million ha area, China being first in area and production (0.90 million ha and 19.05 million tonnes) and India second (0.46 million ha and 6.22 million tonnes as per NHRDF Estimates) during 2005. The productivity of onion is highest in Korea Republic (62.50 tonnes/ha), followed by Ireland (55.92 tonnes/ha), Netherlands (53.33 tonnes/ha), USA (51.19 tonnes/ha), Chile (48.72 tonnes/ha), Japan (47.09 tonnes/ha), Spain (46.34 tonnes/ha) and India (10.38 tonnes/ha). The main reason for low productivity in India is follow up of traditional cultivation methods and use of local varieties. In India, Maharashtra is a leading onion growing state followed by Gujarat, Uttar Pradesh, Orissa, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Karnataka, Bihar, Haryana, Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh.
QUALITY STANDARDS FOR EXPORTS :- The bulbs selected for export should be reasonably uniform in shape, size, colour and pungency. They should be mature, solid, reasonably firm with tough clinging skin, thoroughly cured and dried outer scales free from dirt and other foreign material. Defective, diseased, damaged bulbs caused by seed stem, tops, roots, moisture, dry sunscald, sun burn, sprouting, mechanical or other injuries and staining, free from moulds, soft rot and insect attack should not be used for export. The trained labourer are thus required for grading and packing of onions for exports.
PACKAGING :- The packing size for export varies from 3 to 40 kg depending on requirement of onion by importing country.
